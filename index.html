Jacob R. Gardner

jrg365 (at) cornell (dot) edu

I do research in machine learning, with a focus on Gaussian processes and other Bayesian machine learning methods. My work has primarily focused on techniques for scaling these methods to the large scale problems faced in the real world.

I am currently a postdoctoral associate in Operations Research and Information Engineering at Cornell University working with Andrew Wilson. Prior to this, I received my Ph.D. in Computer Science from Cornell University, where I was advised by Kilian Weinberger.

My Research

Research Interests

Scalable Gaussian Processes

By leveraging modern scientific computing and numerical linear algebra techniques, we can scale Gaussian processes to massive datasets with strong GPU acceleration.

Publications

Structure Exploiting Gaussian Processes

By exploiting problem-specific structure, we can often significantly improve things like the convergence of Bayesian optimization or the dimensionality scaling of scalable kernel interpolation.

Publications

Deep Latent Spaces

The latent spaces of deep neural networks allow us to apply simple transformations and kernel methods to efficiently perform tasks like image transformation scalable Bayesian object recognition.

GPyTorch

Geoff and I founded the GPyTorch project, which aims to implement Gaussian processes in a modular package with strong GPU acceleration. It is deeply embedded in the PyTorch ecosystem, and makes designing complicated models like deep kernel learning both easy to implement and highly efficient. It departs significantly from existing Gaussian process libraries, in that it makes use of modern numerical linear algebra techniques like linear conjugate gradients to perform the fundamental operations required for inference, rather than standard Cholesky based approaches.
